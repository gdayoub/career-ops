## C) Level & Strategy

Google's SWE internship process is one of the most rigorous in tech. For BS interns, expect:
1. **OA or phone screen** — 1–2 LeetCode-style problems (Medium difficulty)
2. **Technical phone/video interviews** — 2–3 rounds focused on DSA (trees, graphs, DP, hashmaps) and sometimes coding style
3. **Host matching** — after passing interviews, matched to a team/project

George is a realistic candidate based on profile — the bottleneck is interview prep, not credentials. Penn State is a recognized feeder school for Google. NLP and AI experience are directly relevant to multiple Google teams (Search, Google Brain, DeepMind, AI Studio).

**Strategic priorities:**
- Heavy LeetCode prep (100+ problems, focus on Medium+, especially graphs, DP, and string manipulation)
- Target host teams with AI/NLP/ML projects during host matching (Google Search, AI Studio, Google DeepMind, Project Zero)
- Apply early — Google's pipeline moves fast and early applications get more host-matching slots

## F) STAR+R Interview Stories

**1. Describe a technically challenging project (Systems Design depth)**
- **S:** LionPlanner needed to handle a combinatorially explosive schedule-generation problem
- **T:** Generate up to 200 conflict-free, constraint-satisfying schedules in under 1 second for Penn State students
- **A:** Designed a backtracking algorithm with early pruning on time conflicts; incorporated multi-criteria scoring (professor rating, GPA safety, workload); scraped LionPATH and RMP using Selenium
- **R:** Deployed on GCP Cloud Run; generates valid schedules in <500ms for typical inputs
- **R:** Would add memoization of partial solutions to handle even larger course catalogs

**2. Tell me about a project that involved ML/NLP (Domain depth)**
- **S:** Needed to classify Arabic-language news by geopolitical topic — no existing labeled dataset for this domain
- **T:** Build and evaluate a classifier from scratch on limited, domain-specific data
- **A:** Built preprocessing pipeline (Unicode normalization, Arabic stopwords, camel-tools morphological analysis), extracted TF-IDF features, trained logistic regression classifier, evaluated with precision/recall
- **R:** Live classifier running in production, integrated with Streamlit dashboard
- **R:** Next step: fine-tune a multilingual BERT variant for improved recall on novel topics

**3. Tell me about a time you built a full-stack product (Scope)**
- **S:** CoverMe needed to go from idea to working SaaS with payments in weeks
- **T:** Build and ship a complete AI-powered SaaS product solo
- **A:** Next.js frontend, Supabase Auth + RLS, Stripe subscription billing, Claude AI backend, 5 LaTeX templates with real-time preview
- **R:** Live on Vercel with real paying users and persistent letter history
- **R:** Shipping solo taught me to make architectural decisions quickly and live with them — would use a monorepo earlier next time

**4. Tell me about solving a problem in an unfamiliar domain (Adaptability)**
- **S:** At Seismos, PJR PDFs had inconsistent structure — standard PDF parsing failed on ~40% of documents
- **T:** Build extraction that worked across all PDF variants without manual intervention
- **A:** Wrote layered Python parsers (text layer first, positional extraction as fallback, regex heuristics for edge cases); validated on a ground-truth subset
- **R:** Automated extraction across all document types; cross-functional engineering teams used output directly
- **R:** Would invest in training a document-layout ML model for the most ambiguous cases

**5. Tell me about a time you collaborated effectively on a technical project (Teamwork)**
- **S:** At Seismos, DS outputs needed to meet engineering team quality standards — not just be technically correct
- **T:** Align on what "correct" meant with non-data-science engineers
- **A:** Set up regular validation checkpoints with the engineering team; created readable Excel reports (not just raw data outputs) with clear column naming and units; wrote documentation for the pipeline
- **R:** Zero quality control failures on client-facing deliverables during the internship
- **R:** Learned that stakeholder communication is part of the DS job, not separate from it
